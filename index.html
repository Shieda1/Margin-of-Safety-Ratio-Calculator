What is the margin of safety?
the margin of safety ratio is the difference between the expected profitablility and the break even point.
How to calculate margin of safety?
To calculate margin of safety you should use the following formula:
margin of safety = ((current sales level - breakeven point) / current sales level) * 100
example: company xh purchased a new machine for $100,000 and after the purchse the company's sales became $390,000 with a break even point of $355,000 what is the marginn of safety?
using the margin of safety formula the answer will be:
margin of safety = ((current sales level - breakeven point) / current sales level) * 100
margin of safety = (($390,000 - $355,000) / $390,000) * 100
margin of safety = 8.97%
Why use margin of safety?
the margin of safety is used to alert the management on the risk of a loss that is about to happen. A high margin of safety indicates protection from sales variations.
